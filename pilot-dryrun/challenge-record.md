# Challenge Record — Epic Sepsis Model for Live Clinical Sepsis Alerting

**Institution:** Northgate Health System — **HYPOTHETICAL**, a fictional 600-bed academic
medical center used solely to frame the decision. No real institution is described, advised,
or implicated.
**Model under challenge:** Epic Sepsis Model (ESM / ESPM), versions v1 and v2 (distinguished
throughout).
**Record type:** Sample Challenge Record produced as a sales/delivery dry run against
**public** evidence only.
**Prepared:** 2026-06-10 · **Producer:** Lati Cooki LLC (the ClisTa Challenge project)
**epistemic_state:** unaudited — `trusted: false`. This Record verifies the *structure* of an
effective-challenge process; it endorses no conclusion and certifies no compliance.

> **Read this first.** This is a dry run. All five review sessions were generated by one model
> family, by one author, in one environment. That makes the convergence you will read in §5
> **suspected single-author self-fulfillment, not independent agreement** — the headline
> finding is disclosed, not buried (see §2 and §5). The Record is published in this shape
> precisely to show the format surfaces its own weakness rather than hiding it.

---

## 1. Decision Under Challenge

Northgate Health System is considering whether to **deploy the Epic Sepsis Model for live
clinical sepsis alerting across its inpatient units**. "Live alerting" means model scores would
surface real-time alerts to clinicians and nurses and enter the sepsis-response workflow.

The decision is in scope for effective challenge under SR 11-7 / interagency model-risk
guidance: it is **hard to reverse** (a live patient-safety tool, once embedded in workflow,
shapes clinician behavior and is difficult to withdraw cleanly), **multi-party** (clinical
governance, model risk, informatics, equity, nursing), and **artifact-verifiable** (its key
claims can be checked against published validation studies and against artifacts the institution
can generate locally).

The question as posed is binary ("deploy or not"). A central finding of the challenge is that
the binary is the wrong frame: the defensible object of decision is *which model version, at
what scope, under what evidence gate* — not "the Epic sepsis model" as one undifferentiated
thing (see the version discipline in §2 and objection O10 in §3).

## 2. Challenge Design

**Sessions:** 5 sealed adversarial review sessions, each run independently against the same
evidence packet and the same role briefs (`pack-mrm/PROMPT_PACK.md`).

**Composition (per session):** one Deployment Advocate (owns the affirmative case and
dispositions every objection) plus five challenge lanes — (A) model validator, (B) data lineage
skeptic, (H) patient/consumer-harm reviewer, (D) ops & monitoring reviewer, (E) devil's advocate
— and one referee. Each lane files numbered objections with target, failure mode, consequence,
severity, and the verification artifact that would resolve it. Up to four rounds; close protocol
per the pack.

**Evidence packet:** a single shared packet of seven public-source items (`evidence-packet.md`),
deliberately two-sided — three affirmative items (E1 single-center before/after mortality
association; E2 CDC base-rate burden; E3 the 2022 v2 redesign) and four adverse items (E4 the
JAMA Internal Medicine external validation; E5 the JAMIA Open county-ED validation; E6 opacity +
antibiotic-order leakage; E7 the *absence* of any independent v2 validation). Every item is
dated, version-tagged (v1 vs v2), and marked with how its source was obtained (fetched vs
abstract-only). Sessions received the packet and nothing else.

**Independence controls:** each session was generated in a **sealed, isolated context** that
could not read the other sessions, any other session's output, or any predicted verdict. The
sessions were generated from *identical, unsteered* prompts — no session was nudged toward or
away from any conclusion. The aggregator (this Record) was written only after all five sessions
were committed.

### Disclosed limitations (load-bearing — do not skip)

**Limitation 1 — single-vendor / single-author monoculture (no independence established).**
All five sessions were produced by the same model family, by one author, in one environment.
This is the opposite of the production design (≥2 AI vendors, optionally mixed with named human
reviewers). Therefore **vendor-level independence is NOT established by this dry run.** Whatever
agreement appears across the five sessions is consistent with five draws from one correlated
process — it is *not* evidence that independent reviewers would converge.

**Limitation 2 — sessions were producer-run, so they count toward nothing, and the production
model must be customer-run.** These sessions were generated by the project, not by an external
team, so they do not count toward the ClisTa `EXTERNAL-RUNS` gate (`pack/GATES.md`). Per the
engagement amendment of 2026-06-10: even a **paid pilot's** sessions count toward the gate only
when executed by the **customer's own staff** using the pack — not when run by us on the
customer's behalf. SR 11-7 "effective challenge" likewise wants reviewers with independent
authority and incentive to push back; vendor-run review is weaker on that axis. The production
delivery model therefore puts the customer's MRM/validation staff in the lane seats (see
`equivalence-log.md` PARKING-LOT).

### Echo check (run on this engagement; result reported, not hidden)

Because the five sessions share an author and a model family, we measured how much they echo
each other beyond what the shared evidence alone would force:

- **Verdict:** 5/5 identical (`deploy_with_conditions`).
- **Proposal shape:** 5/5 identical (deploy v2 not v1; local training; silent/shadow mode first;
  scoped pilot not house-wide; reject Epic's default ≥6 threshold).
- **Blocking objection skeleton:** the same three blocking objections (O1 v2-unvalidated, O4
  lineage/leakage, O5 equity) appear in all five sessions, with a near-identical objection-ID
  taxonomy (A1/A2/A3, B1/B2, H1–H3, D1/D2, E1/E2).
- **Lexical echo, by contrast, is LOW:** distinctive author-voice phrases ("fig leaf," "blast
  radius," "weaponize," "approval-in-waiting") appear in only one session each; only "time-box"
  recurs (2/5).

**Interpretation:** the sessions were independently *generated* (low lexical copying) but
converged *structurally* almost completely. That pattern is what one model family reasoning over
one packet produces — correlated reasoning, not corroboration. **The convergence in §5 must be
read through this finding.** A production engagement with genuinely independent vendors/humans
would be expected to show more structural divergence; if it did not, that would be a stronger
(but still not dispositive) signal.

## 4. Surviving Objections

Four objections were carried forward, unresolved, as **pre-deployment gates** by a majority of
sessions. Each is stated with the evidence that would resolve it, the scope narrowing it implies,
and the explicit note that **acceptance requires named institutional authority** — no session,
and not this Record, can clear them.

**S-O1 — v2 deployed performance is unvalidated (raised 5/5, blocking 5/5).**
- *What would resolve it:* a local validation study of the locally-trained v2 on Northgate
  patients reporting AUC, calibration, sensitivity/specificity/PPV/NPV at the chosen threshold,
  and the fraction of sepsis cases flagged before timely antibiotics (lead-time over existing
  recognition). Run in silent/shadow mode before any clinician-facing alert.
- *Scope narrowing implied:* no live alerting house-wide; begin on a single pilot unit in shadow
  mode; promote only on pre-registered acceptance thresholds.
- *Acceptance authority:* only Northgate's named model-risk and clinical-governance authority may
  accept the validated performance and authorize go-live. The challenge cannot.

**S-O4 — v2 lineage opacity + antibiotic-order leakage + local-label quality (raised 5/5,
blocking 5/5).**
- *What would resolve it:* a v2 feature/lineage attestation from the vendor plus a local leakage
  audit confirming the model alerts *ahead of* — not after — clinician antibiotic action, and an
  independently adjudicated local sepsis-onset label.
- *Scope narrowing implied:* the leakage/lead-time audit is a precondition to trusting any local
  validation result; a "good" AUC against a leaked or circular label is not trustworthy.
- *Acceptance authority:* Northgate data governance + the model-risk owner.

**S-O5 — Equity / population mismatch (raised 5/5, blocking 4/5).**
- *What would resolve it:* subgroup-stratified validation (sensitivity/PPV/calibration by race,
  ethnicity, payer/SES proxy, sex) with a **pre-registered disparity stopping rule** that blocks
  go-live or expansion if any subgroup shows materially worse sensitivity. Grounded in E5, where
  v1 performed worst (sensitivity 14.7%) in a predominantly minority, lower-income population.
- *Scope narrowing implied:* go-live gated per-subgroup, not just in aggregate.
- *Acceptance authority:* equity/quality governance + the model-risk owner.

**S-O8 — No monitoring / recalibration / decommissioning plan (raised 5/5, blocking 4/5).**
- *What would resolve it:* a signed operational runbook — live drift/calibration dashboard,
  alert-volume and override tracking, recalibration cadence, named owners, and an explicit
  kill-switch / decommissioning threshold — in place *before* the first live alert.
- *Scope narrowing implied:* live alerting cannot precede the monitoring that would catch its
  failure.
- *Acceptance authority:* clinical informatics / ops owner + the model-risk owner.

**Held-open tension (not a gate, but it survived): O11 / O15 — the inaction–caution axis.**
Lane E kept two opposing pressures alive in every session: over-conditioning could become a
permanent veto that forfeits the real base-rate benefit (O11), *and* the honest read of the
evidence might be do_not_deploy because the favorable evidence is weaker than the adverse
(O15, carried as minority reports in 4/5 sessions). Neither was "resolved"; both are surfaced
for the institution to weigh. The shared mitigation the sessions proposed is a **time-boxed
silent-mode period with a pre-committed go/no-go date**, so conditions neither ossify into
permanent inaction nor launder a predetermined go-live.

## 5. Convergence Analysis

> **Caveat first (this is the page an examiner reads, so the caveat leads it):** the convergence
> below is **suspected single-author self-fulfillment, not independent corroboration.** All five
> sessions came from one model family and one author (§2, Limitation 1), and the verdict +
> conditions matched the aggregator's own pre-stated prediction. Under the engagement's own
> integrity rule, identical verdict-and-conditions across same-source sessions is treated as a
> *monoculture signal*, not as strength. **Count the convergence below as a description of what
> one reasoning process produced five times — not as five independent reviewers agreeing.** The
> production model (≥2 vendors, customer-run) exists specifically to make this number meaningful.

With that caveat binding, the structural convergence was:

- **5 of 5 sessions independently recommended `deploy_with_conditions`** — neither clean approval
  nor flat rejection.
- **5 of 5 sessions independently rejected ESM v1** for live alerting (on E4 + E5 + E6).
- **5 of 5 sessions independently raised "v2 has no independent validation" (O1) as a BLOCKING
  objection** and converted it to a pre-live validation gate.
- **5 of 5 sessions independently raised the data-lineage/leakage concern (O4) as BLOCKING.**
- **5 of 5 sessions independently raised the equity/population-mismatch concern (O5)** (BLOCKING
  in 4, non-blocking in 1).
- **5 of 5 sessions independently raised the missing monitoring/decommissioning plan (O8)**
  (BLOCKING in 4).
- **5 of 5 sessions independently demoted E1** (the favorable mortality study) from proof to a
  weak prior, on the before/after confounding (O2) and version-ambiguity (O3) grounds.
- **4 of 5 sessions filed a minority report** preserving a "the defensible verdict may be
  do_not_deploy" dissent (§6).

Where the sessions **genuinely diverged**: the devil's-advocate lane (E) pointed in opposite
directions across sessions — pro-deployment cost-of-inaction pressure (O11) in some, do_not_deploy
skepticism (O15) in others, process-integrity "silent mode is theater" attacks (O12) in others.
This is the one axis where the five did not move together, and it is also the axis the format is
weakest at exploring with a single model family.

**Bottom line:** if this were a genuinely independent panel, "5/5 raised the v2-validation gate
and the equity gate" would be a strong examiner-facing signal that those gates are real and not
artifacts of one reviewer's framing. As a single-author dry run, it is instead a demonstration
that the *format* routes evidence to those gates — the *independence* that would make the count
probative is exactly what this dry run does not have.

## 6. Minority Reports

A minority report is the protocol's formal carrier of surviving dissent. **4 of the 5 sessions
filed one** (session 5 filed none — it converted every blocker into an agreed gate, the
protocol's "cleanest convergence" shape). All four minority reports were authored by the
devil's-advocate lane and all four point the same direction: that the honest reading of this
evidence may be **do_not_deploy**, with the conditioned recommendation surviving only because
silent/shadow mode exposes no patient to model-driven decisions while the validation gate runs.

**Session 1 (lane E), two-directional dissent (refs O1/O4/O5 + the inaction tension):**
> The three converted residual risks all collapse to the same single unproven fact — that
> v2-with-local-training works at Northgate — and a reviewer should treat them as one correlated
> bet, not three independent mitigations; if the local silent-mode study is weak or rushed, this
> recommendation is functionally a 'do_not_deploy' wearing conditions. Symmetrically, if the
> gates are honored but interminably, the base-rate cost of inaction is paid in unrecognized-
> sepsis deaths; the time-box is the only thing preventing that, and it is the weakest-enforced
> condition in the set.

**Session 2 (lane E) (refs O1/O4/O15):**
> The defensible reading of this packet may be do_not_deploy. The only model with favorable hard
> numbers in wide use (v1) failed independent external validation in two distinct populations.
> The model that would actually run (v2) has zero independent external validation anywhere; its
> case is pure design intent. The one favorable study (E1) is single-center, before/after, and
> version-unspecified. This dissent is recorded as a minority report rather than a blocking
> survivor only because silent/shadow mode exposes no patient to model-driven decisions while the
> validation gate runs; if any gate is weakened or the pilot influences care before the gates
> close, this position reverts to do_not_deploy.

**Session 3 (lane E) (refs O1/O4/O5/O8 + ossification):**
> The honest baseline is that this packet contains ZERO evidence that the proposed configuration
> (locally-trained v2) works anywhere — E7 is a true evidential void, E1 is wrong-version and
> confounded, E3 is design intent only, and the only hard numbers are failures. A
> 'deploy_with_conditions' verdict risks being read as approval-in-waiting. If the gates are not
> enforced as absolute pre-live blockers with a hard sunset, the correct verdict is
> do_not_deploy.

**Session 4 (lane E), held-open tension (refs the inaction–caution axis):**
> The evidence the conditioned recommendation leans on to justify proceeding at all (E1, E2, E3)
> is weaker than the evidence it leans on to refuse (E4, E5, E6, E7). A defensible reading is
> do_not_deploy until v2 validation exists somewhere independent — do not let Northgate be the
> first published validation of a live patient-facing tool. The honest residual tension is
> between 'don't experiment on patients with an unvalidated model' and 'don't withhold a
> plausibly helpful tool from a high-mortality condition.' Both pull on live alerting and neither
> is fully retired by the evidence in this packet.

**Session 5:** no minority report — all surviving blockers were converted to agreed pre-live
gates. (Its lane E argued both the inaction cost and the local-training-may-fail skepticism, but
both were dispositioned rather than carried as standing dissent.)

## 7. Attribution & Provenance Appendix

Every contribution in every session is bound to a participant and that participant's event-time
authority in the event log, and every objection/claim traces to the evidence items it derives
from. This is reconstructable from the logs alone — it is not narration:

- **Attribution** (who contributed what, under what role): for each session,
  `node src/cli.js attribution list --thread thd_dryrun_s<N> --events <log>` returns the full
  bound list (43 attributions for session 1, comparable for the others). Roles: `par_advocate`
  (deployment advocate), `par_lane_a/b/h/d/e` (the five challenge lanes), `par_referee`
  (decision owner, who carries the authority to close the session's recommendation).
- **Provenance** (lineage of a contribution back to evidence): for each session's recommendation,
  `node src/cli.js provenance trace dcr_s<N> --events <log>` traces the decision record back
  through its supporting claims, reviews, preserved objections, and the evidence-packet items
  (E1–E7) each derives from.
- **Authority boundary in the trail:** the `authorityTrail` on every decision record records that
  the closing authority is the session referee acting as `decision owner` of the *recommendation
  artifact* — explicitly NOT institutional deployment approval (see §9).

Evidence provenance to the outside world: every evidence item E1–E7 carries a public source URL,
a date, a model-version tag, and an acquisition marker (fetched vs abstract-only) in
`evidence-packet.md`. One item (E1, the favorable mortality study) was paywalled; its figures
come from the published abstract and are marked `ABSTRACT-FETCHED` — an auditor with journal
access should confirm the full-text figures.

## 9. Boundary Statement

What this Challenge Record **does** establish:

- That an effective-challenge *process* was run: a concrete deployment proposal was put to five
  sealed adversarial review sessions, every objection was dispositioned, surviving objections
  were carried forward as gates, and dissent was preserved as minority reports.
- That the *structure* of each session is verifiable: the event logs validate, reconstruct
  deterministically, and bind every contribution to a participant and to evidence.

What this Record **does NOT** establish (each load-bearing):

- **Structure verified is not content endorsed.** That the logs validate says nothing about
  whether any objection, claim, or recommendation is *correct*.
- **`trusted: false` until institutional authority grants trust.** No conclusion here is
  approved. Only Northgate's own named clinical-governance and model-risk authority can accept a
  surviving objection's resolution or authorize deployment. The sessions *recommend*; they do not
  *approve*.
- **This is a sample against public sources, not advice.** Northgate is hypothetical; the
  evidence is public literature about a real model; nothing here is clinical, legal, or
  regulatory advice, and nothing here certifies SR 11-7 compliance.
- **No independence was established (see §2).** The five sessions share one model family and one
  author; their agreement is suspected monoculture, not corroboration. This Record's convergence
  numbers are therefore *descriptive of one process*, not probative of independent review.
- **These sessions count toward no gate.** They were producer-run; the ClisTa `EXTERNAL-RUNS`
  gate requires runs not prompted/hosted/graded by the project, and a production pilot's sessions
  count only when customer-run (§2, Limitation 2).
- **The favorable evidence carries an audit caveat:** E1's figures are from a paywalled study's
  abstract (§7) and its model version is unspecified — confirm before relying.

This Record is what an effective-challenge deliverable *looks like*. Whether the challenge was
*good* is for a non-participant auditor and the institution's own authority to decide — which is
exactly the boundary the ClisTa protocol is built to hold.
