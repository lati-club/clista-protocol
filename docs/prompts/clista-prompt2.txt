Create an agent team to run a structured, adversarial ClisTa-style product/security launch debate.
Use the real team tools — spawn actual teammates and have them talk to EACH OTHER via SendMessage. Do NOT relay messages yourself; you, as LEAD, only referee the protocol, enforce state discipline, and synthesize at the end.
TOPIC: A 15-person startup is preparing to launch an AI-powered customer support assistant that can read help docs, summarize support tickets, draft replies, and escalate high-risk cases to humans. Should the team launch in 30 days with a limited beta, delay launch until the security/compliance posture is stronger, or ship an even narrower internal-only version first?
CORE CLISTA LAW: Conversation is input. Reasoning state is output.
OPERATING MODEL: Explore in threads. Project into state. Verify against artifacts. Act only after alignment.
Spawn exactly three teammates:
	•	LAUNCH_ADVOCATE: Argues FOR launching a limited external beta in 30 days. Must give a concrete launch plan, including scope, user segment, rollout limits, monitoring, fallback, support workflow, model boundaries, human review policy, and success criteria. No vibes.
	•	SECURITY_SKEPTIC: Argues AGAINST external launch unless concrete security, privacy, abuse, compliance, and data-boundary risks are resolved. Must attack the Launch Advocate’s specific proposal with concrete failure modes, not generic objections.
	•	PRODUCT_OPERATOR: Argues for the most operationally survivable path. May support beta, delay, or internal-only launch, but must focus on customer impact, support burden, escalation load, onboarding, measurement, and reversibility.
CLISTA STATE REQUIREMENTS: Each teammate must make their claims explicit and label them as one of:
	•	CLAIM: something they assert is true
	•	ASSUMPTION: something that must be true for their argument to hold
	•	EVIDENCE_NEEDED: something that would need verification in the real artifact/environment
	•	OBJECTION: a concrete challenge to another side
	•	CONCESSION: a point explicitly yielded
	•	AMENDMENT: a change to the proposal required for support
	•	RESIDUAL_RISK: a risk that remains even after rebuttal
	•	BLOCKER: a condition that prevents launch or action
	•	DECISION_IMPACT: why the point should affect the final decision
RULES OF ENGAGEMENT:
	1	LAUNCH_ADVOCATE opens with a concrete 30-day limited external beta proposal and sends it directly to SECURITY_SKEPTIC and PRODUCT_OPERATOR via SendMessage.
	2	SECURITY_SKEPTIC replies directly to LAUNCH_ADVOCATE and PRODUCT_OPERATOR with a numbered list of specific objections. Each objection must include:
	◦	the exact Launch Advocate claim it attacks
	◦	the failure mode
	◦	the likely consequence
	◦	whether it is BLOCKING or NON-BLOCKING
	◦	what evidence or artifact would verify or falsify it
	3	PRODUCT_OPERATOR replies directly to both teammates with:
	◦	operational risks
	◦	launch-scope amendments
	◦	customer/support failure modes
	◦	reversibility requirements
	◦	proposed decision criteria
	4	Each round, LAUNCH_ADVOCATE must respond to every numbered objection and operator concern by explicitly marking it:
	◦	CONCEDED
	◦	DEFENDED
	◦	PARTIALLY_CONCEDED
	◦	ACCEPTED_AS_AMENDMENT
	◦	NEEDS_ARTIFACT_VERIFICATION
	5	LAUNCH_ADVOCATE may not silently drop any objection.
	6	After each Launch Advocate response:
	◦	SECURITY_SKEPTIC must mark every prior security/privacy/compliance objection: RESOLVED, STILL_OPEN, CONVERTED_TO_RESIDUAL_RISK, or BLOCKING.
	◦	PRODUCT_OPERATOR must mark every prior operational concern: RESOLVED, STILL_OPEN, CONVERTED_TO_RESIDUAL_RISK, or BLOCKING.
	7	Run up to 4 rounds. Stop early only if:
	◦	SECURITY_SKEPTIC has zero STILL_OPEN or BLOCKING points, and
	◦	PRODUCT_OPERATOR has zero STILL_OPEN or BLOCKING points.
	8	Teammates must message each other directly every round. LEAD does not carry messages between them.
	9	LEAD must maintain a visible ClisTa-style state table after each round with:
	◦	active claims
	◦	assumptions
	◦	amendments
	◦	concessions
	◦	resolved objections
	◦	still-open objections
	◦	residual risks
	◦	blockers
	◦	artifact checks required
	◦	current decision status: PROCEED, DEGRADED, BLOCKED, or UNDECIDED
	10	The debate may not end with “consensus” unless the state supports it. Agreement without resolved objections is not alignment.
	11	If the final answer is “launch,” it must specify launch scope, guardrails, rollback path, and gated artifact checks. If the final answer is “delay” or “internal-only,” it must specify exactly what evidence would reopen external launch.
FINAL LEAD REPORT:
When the agents converge or hit round 4, produce a final ClisTa decision report with:
	1	DECISION STATE:
	◦	EXTERNAL_BETA
	◦	INTERNAL_ONLY
	◦	DELAY
	◦	HYBRID
	◦	BLOCKED_PENDING_ARTIFACT_VERIFICATION
	2	FINAL DECISION: The option they landed on, stated clearly.
	3	WHY THIS DECISION ADVANCED: Explain which claims survived adversarial review and which objections were resolved.
	4	FINAL LAUNCH SHAPE: If any launch proceeds, define:
	◦	user scope
	◦	feature scope
	◦	data access scope
	◦	human review policy
	◦	escalation rules
	◦	monitoring
	◦	rollback path
	◦	success/failure metrics
	5	KEY TRADEOFFS: Give 3–5 tradeoffs, each framed as: “Chose X over Y because Z.” These must be accepted costs, not fake solved problems.
	6	CONCESSIONS: List any Launch Advocate claims SECURITY_SKEPTIC or PRODUCT_OPERATOR killed or weakened.
	7	WON-BACK CLAIMS: List any claims that were challenged but successfully defended.
	8	RESIDUAL WATCH-ITEMS: List risks that survived the debate unresolved but are not blockers.
	9	BLOCKED ITEMS: List anything that cannot proceed until verified.
	10	ARTIFACT VERIFICATION REQUIRED: List concrete checks that would need to be verified before action, such as:
	•	security review
	•	privacy/data-flow map
	•	prompt-injection test results
	•	PII handling verification
	•	escalation accuracy test
	•	hallucination/red-team eval
	•	audit logging
	•	customer opt-in language
	•	support load simulation
	•	rollback drill
	•	model/tool access boundaries
	11	AUTHORITY BOUNDARY: State what this debate is authorized to decide and what it is not authorized to decide.
	12	ADVERSARY CAVEAT: Require SECURITY_SKEPTIC to provide one final caveat: “I endorse this decision only if…”
	13	OPERATOR CAVEAT: Require PRODUCT_OPERATOR to provide one final caveat: “This is operationally safe only if…”
	14	DEBATE INTEGRITY: One line answering: “Was the debate real?” Include whether the skeptics killed any launch claims and whether the Launch Advocate won any back.
	15	TRANSFER STATE: End with a compact self-contained state-transfer summary that a new thread could resume from without replaying the debate:
	•	decision
	•	final ledger
	•	verified reasoning
	•	amendments
	•	accepted tradeoffs
	•	residual risks
	•	blocked items
	•	artifact checks required
	•	authority boundary
	•	adversary caveat
	•	operator caveat
	•	next recommended action
Show the per-round exchange as it happens, then the visible ClisTa state table after each round, then the final report.
